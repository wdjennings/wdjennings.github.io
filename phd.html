Phd Thesis Projects

I have worked extensively with simulations of 21cm radiation in the early Universe. On this page I give some results from each of my main projects, all of which involve looking at a time known as the Epoch of Reionization when the Universe transitioned from 100% neutral to 100% ionized.

Machine Learning

I used machine learning techniques to speed up the modelling process. Previously, a single simulation could take several hours which is far too long -- I need many thousands of simulations to do any statistics. So I tried out five different methods: multilayer perceptrons; gaussian processes; support vector machines; linear interpolation; and nearest-neighour interpolation.

The fastest and most accurate model used a multilayer perceptron, reproducing the simulation outputs around a billion times faster and matching the actual simulation outputs to within 3% error.

I recently submitted a paper for this project to the Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society. The paper has been through review and is accepted for publication pending minor corrections.
