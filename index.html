Scientific Modelling: 21cm Radiation

I developed and tested my own analytic theory of radiation in the early Universe. My model makes fast predictions concerning the pockets of 21cm-wavelength radiation which grow around young galaxies.

Machine Learning Techniques

I used machine learning techniques to reduce the time and cost of running simulations, both of which are crucial limiting factors for statistical analysis. My trained model is around a billion times faster than the full simulation, and mimics the outputs with less than 3% loss in accuracy: the graph below shows a range of predictions both from my model (dotted lines) and the full simuatlions (solid lines).

Bayesian Inference & MCMC

I'm using Bayesian inference with Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to investigate one of the least understood parts of the Universe's history: the Epoch of Reionization. 21cm radiation is a great way to probe these 'Dark Ages'.

Master's Thesis: Disease Epidemics

I developed a simulation for how a disease spreads across a city, extending the Susceptible-Infected-Removed model for Measles, Mumps and Rubella. By running my simulation many times, a newly discovered disease can be classified as 'invasive' (likely to cause an epidemic) or non-invasive.
