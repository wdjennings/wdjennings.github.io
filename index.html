Will Jennings

I'm a research scientist, currently finishing my PhD in the Cosmology group at University College London. I have master's and bachelor's degrees in Physics from the University of Cambridge.

I'm currently developing and analysing early-Universe simulations, using Bayesian inference and machine learning to improve our understanding of what the Universe looked like over 12 billion years ago.

Machine Learning Techniques

I used machine learning to increase the speed of our simulations dramatically. My trained model is around a billion times faster than the full simulation, with less than a 3% loss in accuracy. This graph shows some predictions both from my model (dotted lines) and the full simuatlions (solid lines).

Scientific Modelling: 21cm Radiation

I created and tested my own mathematical theory of radiation in the early Universe. My model makes fast predictions about pockets of 21cm-wavelength radiation which grow around young galaxies, such as those in the image shown here.

Master's Thesis: Disease Epidemics

I developed a simulation for how a disease spreads across a city, extending the Susceptible-Infected-Removed model for Measles, Mumps and Rubella. My simulation allows newly discovered diseases to be classified as 'invasive' (likely to cause an epidemic) or non-invasive.
