Astrophysics PhD Student

I develop and analyse early-Universe simulations in my research, using Bayesian inference and machine learning to improve our understanding of how the Universe looked over 13 billion years ago. If you're interested in scientific modelling or have a joint project idea, then get in touch.

Machine Learning

Modelling speed is a crucial limiting factor for statistical analysis. A single simulation lasts several hours which is far too long -- I need many thousands of simulations to get a decent comparison with real data. So I've recently used machine learning techniques to speed things up: my trained model is around a billion times faster than the full simulation, and mimics the outputs with less than 3% loss in accuracy!

Mathematical Model

I'm developing my own mathematical theory of what 21cm radiation observations will look like, for many different possible models of the early universe. Observing radiation from 13 billion years ago is very difficult. Excitingly, we're expecting huge volumes of high quality data from upcoming experiments like the SKA and the HERA. I can use my mathematical theory with the data from such experiments to find out which of early-universe model is most likely.

Bayesian Inference & MCMC
